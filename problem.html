Mendon House

Problem Statement

Since Mendon House closed in 2003, the recipes from my grandfather’s restaurant have been unavailable for anyone outside of our family. With the passing of my grandfather 2 years ago, and since gaining possession of all his recipes, now felt like the perfect time to organize and better preserve his recipes and the family history.

These recipes are loosely bound, have zero instruction for the average home cook, and are scaled for a full night’s service in a restaurant. My inspiration comes directly from the collection of recipes that we have. These are foods that my family still regularly eats and enjoys cooking. Because instruction has been passed down from one generation to the next, most processes for making these meals is very innate.

This project is an exploration of food as heritage by cataloging these recipes into an accessible and approachable method for any level cook.
